Butterfly Pea Flower Swirl Sourdough Bread

Daily Grains

Prep Time: 1 hour Cook Time: 40 minutes Source: breadtopia.com

Ingredients

Blue Dough

107 grams Sonora

160 grams bread flour

217 grams blue tea

47 grams sourdough starter

5.33 grams salt

6.66 grams dried whole butterfly pea flowers, for the tea


White Dough

107 grams Sonora

160 grams bread flour

217 grams water

47 grams sourdough starter

5.33 grams salt

Directions

Mixing and Bulk Fermentation

Mix the ingredients of each dough in separate bowls and cover. Over the next 1-2 hours, do four rounds of gluten development (stretching and folding, coil folding, etc.) with 20-30 minute rests in between.

Let the doughs rise until expanded by 75-90%. This could take 4-8 hours, depending on the doughs' starting temp (warm liquids) and the ambient temp. My doughs rose for 6 hours in a 70°F kitchen.

Pre-shaping and Shaping

Flour your work surface and turn out both doughs onto it. Make sure you have several inches of space between the doughs.

Stretch each dough into a rectangle, then place one on top of the other. Fold in thirds to layer the colors and create a long rectangle. Bring the short edges of the rectangle together and flip the dough, tucking the seams underneath and forming a ball.

Cover with your bowl and let rest 15-20 minutes.

Shape the dough and place it seam-side up in a floured proofing basket.

Final Proof

Cover the dough and let it rise again for about an hour at room temperature, then in the refrigerate overnight (8-16 hours). If the ambient temperature is under 70°F, leave the dough at room temperature longer before refrigerating. Or if you prefer to bake the same day, proof for several hours at room temperature. See the target dough expansion in the photo gallery.

Scoring and Baking

Preheat your oven and baking vessel to 500°F for 30 minutes.

Flip the dough out of the proofing basket and onto a sheet of parchment paper and score it.

Bake at

500°F for 20 minutes, lid on

450°F for 20 minutes, lid off

If using a cast iron Dutch oven, you may want to slide a baking sheet directly under the vessel when the lid comes off to prevent the base of the bread from burning.

When baking is complete, the bread should have an internal temperature over 205°F and sound hollow when you knock on the base.

Let the bread cool for a couple of hours before you slice it.

Notes

Liquid Prep

Make the tea for the blue dough by boiling about 160 grams of water. Pour the water over the flowers, cover and let steep 10 minutes. Strain out the flowers, making sure you press out as much blue liquid as you can. Re-weigh the tea and add water as needed to bring it up to 163 grams. Let cool to under 100°F.

Prepare 163 grams of plain water for the white dough. Use some hot water if needed so it's the same temperature as the blue tea. This way the two doughs will ferment at the same pace.
