Whole Wheat and Rye Chocolate Chip Cookies

Daily Grains

Prep Time: 20 minutes Cook Time: 10 minutes Servings: Servings 30-40 cookies Source: breadtopia.com

Ingredients

250 grams hard white whole grain flour (2 cups)

100 grams bono rye whole grain flour (3/4 cups)

6 grams salt (1 tsp)

5 grams baking soda (1 tsp)

228 grams unsalted butter, softened (1 cup)

160 grams brown sugar (~ 3/4 cup)

140 grams white sugar (~ 3/4 cup)

2 eggs

15 grams water (1 Tbsp)

4 grams vanilla extract (1 tsp)

340 grams semisweet chocolate chips (2 cups)

optional 125 grams chopped nuts (1 cup)

Directions

Whisk together the flours, baking soda, and salt in a bowl and set aside.

In a stand mixer bowl, beat the softened butter and sugars with the paddle attachment until creamy.

Slowly add the eggs, water, and vanilla extract. Beat until the ingredients are blended.

Pause the mixer, add the dry ingredients, and mix on slow speed until there are no dry bits.

Add the chocolate chips and optional nuts. Briefly mix them into the dough.

Cover and refrigerate the cookie dough for at least 30 minutes. This step is important for hydrating the whole grain flours, making the dough (which contains sticky rye) easier to handle, and chilling the butter in the dough results in puffier cookies.

About fifteen minutes before you're ready to bake the cookies, preheat your oven to 350Â°F.

Scoop out the cookie dough in 1-2 Tbsp size balls. Try to make each ball about the same size for even baking. Arrange on your baking sheet with about 2 inches of space between each ball.

Bake for 8-12 minutes depending on the cookie size. They should be golden in color with no wet-looking dough on the top.

Remove the baking sheet from the oven, let cool for a couple of minutes, and then transfer the cookies to a cooling rack.

Repeat until all the cookies are baked.

Store in a covered container for about a week. For a tender cookie with melty chocolate, microwave on a plate for 15 seconds before eating.
