Brown Butter Dark Chocolate Chip Cookies

Cookies, Daily Grains, Dessert

Prep Time: about 30 minutes Servings: 7 large cookies Source: barriobread.com

Ingredients

14 tablespoons butter

98 g white sugar

158 g brown sugar

1 1/4 teaspoons salt

2 teaspoons vanilla extract

1 egg

1 egg yolk

260 g Turkey Red/White Sonora/Yecora Rojo 1:1:1 blend

½ teaspoon baking soda

160 g chocolate chips (FREEZE THE CHIPS)

Directions

Preheat oven to 375°.

Melt the butter and let it boil for 3-5 minutes.

Transfer to a mixing bowl and mix in the sugars, salt, vanilla, egg yolk and egg.

In a separate bowl, combine the flour and baking soda then stir into the butter mixture and add the chips.

Mix all until combined.

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

Form dough into golf ball-size for large cookies. Refrigerate as balls before baking.

Bake for 14 minutes - do not overbake. (Standard cookie size baked for 10 minutes with 10 minutes of dough bowl in refrigerator.)

Cool and serve.

Notes

Brown the butter for the full five minutes. Two separate things to try: either chill dough for 30 minutes pre-formed or bake at 400 degrees convection (375).

“For every stick of butter that you’re browning, try to let the butter cool down a bit and then add one tablespoon of water to the dough. It’ll get you closer to the texture of the original.”
