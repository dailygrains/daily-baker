Chia Pudding Sourdough Bread

Daily Grains

Prep Time: 20 minutes Cook Time: 35 minutes Source: breadtopia.com

Ingredients

Dough

500 grams Yecora Rojo whole grain flour or hard white spring wheat whole grain flour (3 3/4 cups)

400 grams water (1 2/3 cups)

60 grams sourdough starter (scant 1/4 cup)

10 grams salt (2 tsp)

Chia Pudding

50 grams chia seeds (1/3 cup)

120 grams water (1/2 cup)

Directions

Combine the chia seeds and water in a small bowl. In another bowl (or stand mixer with the dough hook attachment), mix the dough ingredients. Cover both bowls and let sit for about an hour.

Add the chia pudding to the dough and mix for about 5 minutes. If using a stand mixer, mix on low-med speed. By hand, you can slap and fold the dough or use the rubaud technique. Both methods are described in this gluten development article.

Let the dough rise until it has expanded by about 50%. If the ambient temperature is hot, this may take as little as 4 hours from initial mixing and you may want to stop at even less than 50% expansion. In cooler temps, the bulk fermentation could take as long as 9 hours.

Shape the dough and place it in a well-floured proofing basket. If the temps are cool and/or the dough feels a little under-fermented, you can pre-shape the dough and give it a 20-30 minute bench rest before shaping. It's also a good idea to pre-shape and rest dough if you scaled up the recipe and have to divide the dough for multiple loaves.

Cover and let the dough expand either on the counter at room temperature for 1-3 hours, in the refrigerator for 8-12 hours, or in a combination of both places e.g. 30 minutes at room temperature and overnight in the refrigerator. Also, remember you can put the dough in the freezer for 20-30 minutes just before baking if the ambient temp is very hot.

Preheat your oven and baking vessel to 500F for 30 minutes.

Flip the dough onto a sheet of parchment paper or directly on the base of your baking vessel. Score and transfer it to the baking vessel, cover, and place in the oven.

20 minutes at 500F, lid on

15 minutes at 450F, lid off

The internal temperature of the bread should be over 205F when the bread is done.
