Einkorn Chocolate Mint Chunk Cookies (with Sourdough)

Daily Grains

Prep Time: 45 minutes Cook Time: 10 minutes Servings: Servings 42 Source: breadtopia.com

Ingredients

1 cup home-milled whole grain einkorn wheat berries or whole grain einkorn flour or (135g)

1/2 tsp baking soda

1/4 tsp salt

3/4 cup brown sugar (185g)

6 Tbsp softened unsalted butter (86g)

1 Tbsp sourdough starter (20-40g)

2 eggs

1 tsp vanilla

1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips, melted (6.5 oz / 185g)

10 oz mint chocolate chunks (1 bag / 283g / 1 1/2 cups)

Directions

Cookie Dough

Whisk together in a bowl the flour, baking soda and salt. Set aside.

Melt the semi-sweet chocolate and set aside.

Cream the butter, brown sugar and sourdough starter, using the paddle attachment of a stand mixer if you have one. After the mixture is light and fluffy, add the eggs one at a time and then the vanilla extract.

Slowly add the melted chocolate to the mixer, beating on low speed.

After the chocolate is incorporated, mix in the whisked dry ingredients until they're just incorporated.

Finally, mix in the mint chocolate chunks.

Cover and refrigerate the cookie dough, overnight if possible for some fermentation and at least 30 minutes to make the dough easier to handle.

Preheat your oven to 375F.

Using a cookie dough scooper (1.5" diameter) or a spoon, scoop out a portion of cool cookie dough. If the dough breaks apart, shape it into a ball with damp fingertips.

Lay the dough balls on a baking sheet about 2 inches apart.

Bake 8-10 minutes, just until the edges of the cookies have set.

Cool on a rack. Put in a covered container after the cookies are cool.

Homemade Mint Chocolate Chips

Melt 10 ounces of chocolate in a double boiler on low heat, or in the microwave in a glass container (e.g. Pyrex).

If microwaving, use time intervals of 15-30 seconds and stir in between. I usually stop before every lump is melted, and the final stirring completes the melting.

When the chocolate is melted, add peppermint extract to taste and mix thoroughly.

Spread the chocolate on a sheet of parchment and cut it into chunks after it has cooled.
