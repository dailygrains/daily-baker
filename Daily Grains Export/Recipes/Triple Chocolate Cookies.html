Triple Chocolate Cookies

Cookies, Daily Grains, Dessert

Servings: 6.07 142-gram cookies or 20 42-gram cookies

Ingredients

94g dark rye flour

2T cocoa powder

1/4 t baking powder

1/2 t salt

85g unsalted butter

175g bittersweet chocolate chips (56%, not 70% as they won’t flatten otherwise)

145g white chocolate chips (on 1/21, i did 350g and had 69g leftover, which would be 140g for a single batch AND ON 8/21, we did 2100 and had 350 leftover, which would be 145g per batch)

200g (1 c) sugar

2 eggs

1/2 t vanilla

Directions

Sift the rye flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt into a bowl.

Place the butter and the bittersweet chocolate chips in a large heat-resistant bowl and melt slowly, stirring with a spatula periodically.

In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, combine the melted chocolate mixture with the sugar on low speed.

Add the eggs one at a time, mixing briefly after each addition.

Add the vanilla and the sifted ingredients, continuing to mix on low speed until a uniform dough forms.

Stir in the white chocolate chips and mix just to combine.

Transfer the dough to a separate container, cover, and refrigerate for at least an hour or overnight.

Preheat the oven to 350°F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.

Remove the dough from the refrigerator and shape into 5-oz/142-g balls. Place the cookies onto the prepared sheets.

Bake for 12 minutes, then rotate, then bake for 12 more minutes, then check and possibly bake for 2 more minutes. (The ideal cookie will be set around the edges with a ofter, almost underdone center.) Remove and let cool completely. (Test cookie was 26 minutes at this size.)
