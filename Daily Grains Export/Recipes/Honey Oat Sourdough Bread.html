Honey Oat Sourdough Bread

Daily Grains

Prep Time: 40 minutes Cook Time: 35 minutes Source: breadtopia.com

Ingredients

203 grams hard red spring wheat flour

68 grams oat flour

270 grams bread flour

394 grams water

108 grams sourdough starter

36 grams honey

11 grams salt

Rolled or flakes oats for topping the crust

Total: 1090g

Description

UPDATED 9/18/25 to reflect honey/levain/salt updates

Directions

Mixing and Bulk Fermentation

Thoroughly mix all the ingredients in a bowl and form the dough into a ball with damp hands or with a dough scraper. If available, transfer the dough to a lighly oiled straight-walled bucket. Cover and let the dough rest for 20-30 minutes.

Do a round of stretching and folding of the dough. Cover and rest the dough again for 20-30 minutes, then do a second round of stretching and folding. You can do more rounds of stretching and folding if you'd like.

Cover the dough and let it rise until it has grown by about 75%. See photo in gallery below.

Pre-Shape, Shape, Topping

On a lightly floured countertop, preshape the dough into a tight ball. Cover and let it rest about 20 minutes.

Set up a plate with rolled or flaked oats spread out in the shape you're planning for your dough.

Shape your dough, and brush it lightly with water. Flip the dough wet-side down onto the oats and roll it around to fully coat most of the dough.

Transfer it to your proofing basket (oats down) and add more oats around the perimeter of the dough to make sure it won't stick to the basket.

Final Proof

Cover and let the shaped dough rise again for 1-2 hours at room temperature. For a more sour bread, refrigerate the dough overnight after 20-40 minutes at room temperature. If your kitchen/dough is very hot, you might want to refrigerate immediately; or if very cold, refrigerate after closer to 1 hour. See the photo gallery below for target dough expansion in the proofing basket.

Flip the dough out of the proofing basket and onto a sheet of parchment paper or onto the base of your hot baking vessel. Snip a scoring pattern into the dough with scissors, then cover and return the vessel to the oven.
